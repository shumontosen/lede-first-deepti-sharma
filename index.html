How good is India's Deepti Sharma, the bowler?

She recently became women's international cricket's highest wicket taker. Where does she stand among the world's best?

Sumanta Sen

Deepti Sharma tossed up one on middle and leg stump and Australia's Beth Mooney fell for it. Her attempted slog had height but no distance. Radha Yadav held on to the catch at long-on giving Deepti her 356th international wicket. She now had the most wickets in international cricket, surpassing her former teammate and world legend Jhulan Goswami.

In a career spanning nearly 12 years, she has picked up 356 wickets across all formats, the most in the world. She and Goswami are the only two Indians in the all-time tally of the 15 highest wicket-takers. Among those currently playing, Australia's Ellyse Perry and England's Sophie Ecclestone are not far behind Deepti.

Having played most of her international career in T20 Internationals, it is understandable that she has the most wickets in the format. Having played in only 10 Test innings, she has 22 wickets to her name in the format.

She is a Test elite

Here is where it gets interesting.

In the 10 Test innings that she has bowled in, she has picked up 22 wickets. Among bowlers who have picked up at least 20 wickets in the format she is among the best. Let's unpack this a bit.

In Test matches how good a bowler is depends on the number of balls they bowl per wicket, also called as strike rate, and the number of runs they concede per wicket, also known as average. The lesser these two values, the better the bowler is. In a scatter-plot that shows both these values for each bowler, Deepti features among the world's elite. This conclusion was arrived after dividing the chart into four regions based on the mean value for all players in the chart.

Not among the elite in limited overs format

What makes a good bowler in limited overs cricket is their strike rate and how many runs they concede in an over, also known as the economy rate. Again, the lesser these two values, the better.

Here though, she is outside the group of the best in the world. While her strike rate is better than the mean of all bowlers in both ODIs and T20Is, her economy rate higher. This means that she concedes more runs per over compared to the average of other bowlers in both limited overs format.

The need for more Test cricket?

Since COVID-19 pandemic restrictions eased and sports reponed, limited overs cricket, particularly T20Is in the women's game, have been played the most. Only a handful of Test matches have been played. Moreover, they have been played among only four countries.

In 2016, Goswami had told Sportstar magazine that, "To me Test cricket is the ultimate". In 2021 she told Cricinfo that, "We are all excited because Test cricket or days' match is one thing you, as a cricketer, wanted to play, wanted to go out there and perform well. It involves a lot of challenge - from your mental strength to your physical strength. The game changes every session."

Deepti's example has showed that is more than what meets the eye for a player and a lot is likely being missed about players since fewer Tests are being played. Maybe now is the time for more Tests.
